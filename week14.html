🌐 Network

  • A network is a system of interconnected devices that exchange data and share resources.
  • Devices include computers, servers, smartphones, printers, and IoT devices.
  • Connections can be wired (cables, fiber optics) or wireless (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth).

🎯 Purpose of Networks

  • Communication – fast information sharing (emails, video calls).
  • Resource sharing – printers, software, and data.
  • Centralized management of devices.
  • Collaboration using shared platforms.
  • Scalability to support growth.

🏠 Network Types

  • PAN – Personal Area Network
  • LAN – Local Area Network
  • MAN – Metropolitan Area Network
  • WAN – Wide Area Network

🏢 LAN (Local Area Network)

  • Connects devices within a limited geographic area such as a home, office, or campus.
  • Privately owned and controlled.
  • Supports very high data transfer speeds.
  • Uses wired (Ethernet) or wireless (Wi-Fi) connections.
  • Common topologies: Star, Bus, Hybrid.

✔ LAN Advantages

  • High speed communication.
  • Cost-effective setup.
  • Centralized resource sharing.
  • Improved collaboration.
  • Easier security management.

❌ LAN Limitations

  • Limited coverage area.
  • Initial setup cost can be high.
  • Requires maintenance.

🌍 WAN (Wide Area Network)

  • Covers very large geographical areas (countries, continents).
  • Interconnects LANs and MANs.
  • May be public (Internet) or private (corporate WAN).
  • Uses fiber optics, satellite, leased lines, MPLS, VPNs.
  • Higher latency due to long distances.

✔ WAN Advantages

  • Global connectivity.
  • Centralized resource access.
  • Supports remote access.
  • High reliability using redundancy.
  • Supports cloud services.

❌ WAN Limitations

  • High cost.
  • Higher latency.
  • Complex management.
  • Security challenges.

🧩 Network Topology

  • Refers to the arrangement of nodes and links in a network.
  • Affects performance, cost, scalability, and fault tolerance.

🚌 Bus Topology

  • All devices connected to a single central cable.
  • Easy and low cost.
  • Main cable failure disrupts entire network.

⭐ Star Topology

  • All devices connected to a central hub or switch.
  • Most common LAN topology.
  • Failure of hub affects entire network.

🔁 Ring Topology

  • Devices connected in a circular loop.
  • Data travels in one direction.
  • Failure of one device can break network.

🕸 Mesh Topology

  • Each device connected to multiple devices.
  • Highly reliable and fault-tolerant.
  • Expensive and complex.

🔀 Hybrid Topology

  • Combination of two or more topologies.
  • Flexible and scalable.
  • Complex to manage.
